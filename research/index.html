Research Overview

Practice that fits real classrooms.

Mission: Reinforceable is being developed as part of dissertation research focused on supporting teachers as they implement individualized, function-based behavior support plans. The tool gives teachers brief, realistic opportunities to rehearse plan-aligned decisions, receive immediate feedback, and build confidence before similar moments happen during the school day. (Sanetti & Kratochwill, 2007; Collier-Meek, Sanetti, & Fallon, 2017)

Play the Beta New Game Intake

Useful practice, grounded in research.

Teachers should not have to wait for a difficult classroom moment to rehearse what a support plan asks them to do. Mission: Reinforceable creates a low-risk space to practice those choices first.

Why this matters

Good behavior plans need real-world implementation support.

A behavior support plan can be well designed and still be hard to use consistently in a busy classroom. Teachers are managing instruction, transitions, peer interactions, and competing needs while also trying to respond to student behavior in ways that match the plan.

Mission: Reinforceable is designed for that gap: the space between knowing what a plan says and being able to use it in the moment. (Collier-Meek et al., 2019; Sanetti & Collier-Meek, 2019; Samudre et al., 2020)

What the game supports

Practice targets the decisions teachers actually have to make.

Plan-aligned decisions

Teachers practice choosing responses that match the student’s behavior support plan, including prevention strategies, replacement behavior support, and consequence strategies.

(Ingram et al., 2005; O’Neill et al., 2015)

Implementation fidelity

The goal is to support teachers in using key plan steps as intended during the routines where support is needed most.

(Sanetti & Kratochwill, 2007; Collier-Meek, Sanetti, & Fallon, 2017)

Brief prompts and feedback

The game uses short prompts, realistic scenarios, and immediate feedback to help teachers contact the plan before real classroom implementation moments.

(Collier-Meek, Fallon, & DeFouw, 2017; Fallon et al., 2022; Johnson et al., 2024)

Meaningful practice

Teachers do more than read reminders. They make choices, see outcomes, and use feedback to refine future responses.

(Gee, 2003; Salen & Zimmerman, 2004; Plass et al., 2015)

How it works

A quick practice loop before the school day unfolds.

1

Prompt

A weekday email reminds the teacher to complete a brief mission before the target routine.

2

Practice

The teacher reads a realistic classroom scenario and chooses among plan-aligned, less optimal, and contraindicated responses.

3

Feedback

The game provides immediate confirmatory or corrective feedback so teachers can rehearse a better response before the moment happens in the classroom. (Noell et al., 1997; Fallon et al., 2015; Massar et al., 2023)

Game-based practice

The game format makes practice active.

Mission: Reinforceable uses game elements to make practice more visible and engaging, not to turn behavior support into entertainment. Mission framing, points, hearts, progress indicators, wizard feedback, and dashboards are used to make teacher choices, accuracy, and feedback easier to see.

The goal is a low-risk practice space where teachers can make mistakes, receive feedback, and rehearse more plan-aligned responses. (Gee, 2003; Salen & Zimmerman, 2004; Sailer & Homner, 2020)

Dissertation study overview

What the dissertation study will examine

The dissertation study will evaluate Mission: Reinforceable using a multiple-probe single-case design across teacher-student dyads. During baseline, teachers continue implementing the student’s existing behavior support plan while receiving coaching-as-usual. During intervention, Mission: Reinforceable is added while coaching-as-usual continues. Maintenance probes will examine whether outcomes continue after Mission: Reinforceable is withdrawn. (Kratochwill et al., 2010; What Works Clearinghouse, 2022)

Baseline

Existing BSP implementation and coaching-as-usual.

Intervention

Mission: Reinforceable added while coaching-as-usual continues.

Maintenance

Follow-up probes after Mission: Reinforceable is withdrawn.

Measures

What the study measures

Teacher fidelity

Are key BSP steps implemented as written?

Student behavior

Does the student’s target behavior change during the same routine?

Usage data

Are teachers completing missions, when, and for how long?

Teacher perception

Does the tool feel acceptable, feasible, usable, and a good classroom fit?

Maintenance

Do outcomes continue after the game-based prompts are removed?

Current status

Current status

Mission: Reinforceable is currently in beta development. The public beta is available for usability and design feedback. The tool should not yet be described as evidence-based or proven effective.

The dissertation study is designed to evaluate whether adding Mission: Reinforceable to existing coaching-as-usual is associated with changes in teacher implementation fidelity, student behavior, maintenance, and teacher perceptions of fit.
